X-ray Computed Tomography

Micro X-ray computed tomography (XCT) is an emerging technology that has found many applications in biology and the study of materials. Synchrotron-based micro XCT has been adopted for the study of adhesive bonding in wood. Here are some of the data sets we collected at Argonne National Laboratory in October of 2014. We used a mechanical testing device developed by Michaela Zauner to capture in situ deformation of wood adhesive bonds.
